The paragraphs below summarise my experience in the campus -

2nd August, 2023 - The day of many emotions. It was raining heavily as I and my parents were heading towards the institute that JOSAA had awarded me. And then after being through the level crossing, we saw the letters on the wall - INDIAN INSTITUTE OF TECHNOLOGY KANPUR, and the same written in hindi on the other side. Then I realised, everything going on was true. During that time, I wasn't happy that I got into some institute of national importance but I was extremely sad that my parents would leave the place soon, without me. It was heartbreaking that I would be on my own from then onwards, that I would have to use the new phone of mine to talk to them and that I wouldn't be sleeping to my father's "Good night!" and waking up to my mother's "It's time to wake up!". We had to wait at the checkpoints. Then, we headed to Hall-4 - the fresher-girls' hostel of IITK. That was a long way from the main gates, giving us such a pleasant view of nature that I hadn't seen in my life!

My shelter for the first year. So, as we entered the place, there was a lot of hustle and bustle going on. New cycles, mattresses, tables, buckets... There was a huge gathering - vendors, insurance agents, confused families, sad girls, happy girls, girls with ID-cards around their necks, who looked the calmest of all and dogs, who were habituated with the chaos going on. I called my Student Guide (who would later introduce herself as my 'Amma'). She took me and 5 other girls to the hall office for the room allocation. There was a lot of paper work and digital work done after getting into the single-room-turned-double-room. We got introduced to the Pingala website which would be our asset for the 4 years. We got into the mess for lunch. I don't remember the menu which was served on that day but the quality of food was good. When it was around 8pm, my parents had to leave. Not just mine but every girl's family had to leave by 8pm. It was the rule. It was the saddest moment of my life. Me and my parents were in tears, with my SG (Student Guide) consoling us in every way she could. That night, I had to sleep in an unfamiliar place, on an unfamiliar bed with an unfamiliar girl in the room. I knew that I couldn't escape to my parents' arms anymore if I felt bad in any sense. I don't even remember if I had slept that night or not. That night, I was tired. My mind couldn't process the flurry of things that had happened till then. And there was a lot more to come...

Getting introduced... From the next day onwards, it was our orientation, where they take you throughout the campus and give you lots of important information in one go that you can hardly understand. Of course, some people did but I, personally, didn't feel I was getting it. We were taken to the places in long queues with our Ammas shouting at us to run, else, we'll miss the show. The roads were clean, which is always a rare sight in Delhi. The hot and humid weather was boring. We were taken to the auditorium. There were students from all over India. There were different sorts of things going on the stage - speeches by the important people, club orientations and showcase of talents. We had a bus ride within the campus to get aquainted with the places. It was like a whirlpool for me. The airstrip is fascinating but the story that they recite there was eerie. The sky-photo at the end was the prettiest part of the orientation. With so much of success stories in the air and the feel of "You are in a world of fantasy where every dream of yours comes true..." that everyone (other than the freshers) gives you, you feel that in the 4 years to come, you will be the person you had always dreamed to be and maybe more.

"IITK ka tempo high hai!" As soon as the families leave, you are introduced to some of the IITK's culture. Rest, you learn as the days pass by. Me? I am still learning! There is a family culture in IITK where a sophomore is appointed an SG who is in-charge of 5-6 freshers. In females, the SG is called 'Amma' and the freshers under her are her 'Bachchiyaan', who among themselves are 'Behenein'. In males, the SG is called 'Bapu', the juniors 'Bachchein' and they are 'Bhai' to each other. Similarly, you have 'Nani'/'Dada', 'Mausi'/'Chacha' etc. From the beginning of orientation, they teach you some slogans like - 'IITK ka tempo high hai!', 'Hall 4 ka tempo high hai!', 'Y23 ka tempo high hai!' etc., depending on whom or what you want to glorify, 'Ek do, ek do; Core Team ko fek do!' and so on. You also get to learn some IITK lingo like Chaapu, Maggu, BT, Dassa to name a few. The most popular phrase that you would hear is 'Chill hai!', no matter how insane the situation is! You can think this phrase to be equivalent to 'All is well!' from 3 Idiots

The first day of class and you don't know where to go... The seniors don't guide you to the lecture halls and the labs. You have to use Pingala and Google Maps. When I was about to start for my first class of IIT life, I was lucky enough to get a friend who accompanied me. I was never a pro in using Google Maps. I would get lost in the campus in my hunt for labs or other venues. It took me much longer to memorise the directions than my batchmates. 1055-acres land looks crazier than it sounds, at least when you get lost having nobody with you. Honestly, there were situations, when I avoided events just because I didn't know the location! Of course, people help you but at one point, you would want to do it on your own. You know, I have discovered some amazing routes myself. And it was really fun!

Yes, it happens! In your school, you might be a topper. You might have aced JEE. But the truth is, everyone here has. In an IIT, everyone is an IITian. Everyone was the topper in their school. In an IIT, your competition is with other IITians - the toppers from all across the country! The academics here is, to be honest, tough. With too many courses to master simultaneously in a very short time with a grading that isn't satisfactory, the academics is tough. You have labs, quizzes, mid-sems, end-sems and assignments. That really is a lot! The course stuff is hard to understand but if you have studied thoroughly for olympiads, JSTSE, NTSE, KVPY, JEE etc. before coming to this place, you can easily master the courses here.

IITK offers a lot of things that are so good! The facilities that I have come across till now are great. One of them being the security here. The guards are always on their feet to ensure that the campus is safe and secure. The labs are of course counted in the list of the best ones in India. The library is huge. There are clubs for every kind of field you can imagine. The campus offers sports of every kind. There are eateries and parks and shops that have almost everything that you would require for your entire stay. The Counselling Service is also amazing. It really guides you in the best possible manner and tries to solve whatever problem you face. In terms of facilities, I think IITK is phenomenal.

People are as you perceive them. Once you come here, you will find that the people here, especially, the seniors and elders, are very friendly and always ready to help. Anything you need, they won't leave you clueless. Here, as I have mentioned before, you will meet people from all over India. There is a huge population of the Bengalis and the Telugus. You get to interact with different people, learn about their culture and state. This is really interesting! You will get fascinated by knowing how talented people can be! You would also meet people who don't match your taste and you might not like them but again, there are people who become your best friends! I have met some amazing people here, who have enriched my experience here in some way or the other.
